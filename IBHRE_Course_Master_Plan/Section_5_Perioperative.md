## 5.B. Lead Implantation

> [!example] Video Breakdown: 6 videos, ~65 minutes

### Video 5: Atrial Lead Placement (10-12 min)
- **Right atrial appendage** (RAA) - most common
- Active vs passive fixation choice
- Achieving adequate P-wave (>2mV)
- Fluoroscopy views (RAO, LAO)

### Video 6: RV Lead Placement - Apex (10-12 min)
- Traditional apex position
- Fluoroscopy landmarks (LAO: tip pointing left, RAO: anterior)
- Threshold testing (<1.0V ideal)
- Impedance check (400-1200Ω)

### Video 7: RV Lead Placement - Septum/RVOT (10-12 min)
- Alternative sites (more physiologic)
- Septal position: LAO view (tip points rightward)
- RVOT: RAO view (tip points upward)
- Benefits: Less dyssynchrony

### Video 8: LV Lead Placement for CRT (12-15 min)
- **Coronary sinus cannulation** (engagement angle)
- **Target veins**: Lateral, posterolateral (best electrical delay)
- **Avoid**: Anterior veins (worsen dyssynchrony)
- **Phrenic nerve testing** (high output pacing, observe diaphragm)
- CS dissection risk

### Video 9: ICD Lead Placement (10-12 min)
- RV coil position (across tricuspid valve)
- SVC coil position (superior vena cava)
- DFT testing (historical, not routine now)
- R-wave amplitude (>5mV required for sensing)

### Video 10: Epicardial Lead Placement (8-10 min)
- Indications: Congenital heart disease, no venous access, lead extraction
- Surgical approach (mini-thoracotomy, VATS)
- Higher thresholds (1.5-3.0V)
- Higher risk of exit block

## 5.C. Intraoperative Testing

> [!example] Video Breakdown: 4 videos, ~35 minutes

### Video 11: Threshold Testing Protocol (10-12 min)
**Procedure:**
1. Start high output (5.0V @ 0.4ms)
2. Decrease in 0.5V steps
3. Identify threshold (loss of capture)
4. Program output = threshold × 2

**Acceptable thresholds:**
- Atrium: <1.5V
- RV pacing lead: <1.0V
- LV lead: <2.5V (higher acceptable)

### Video 12: Sensing Testing (8-10 min)
- **P-wave**: >2.0mV (ideal >3mV)
- **R-wave**: >5.0mV (ICD requires >5mV)
- **Slew rate**: Rate of voltage change (important for ICDs)

### Video 13: Impedance Measurements (8-10 min)
- Pacing leads: 400-1200Ω (optimal 600-900Ω)
- ICD coils: 25-80Ω
- Out-of-range → check connections, lead integrity

### Video 14: Fluoroscopy & Lead Positioning (10-12 min)
- **RAO view**: Anteroposterior position
- **LAO view**: Septal vs free wall
- Lead slack (gentle J-curve, prevents dislodgement)
- Final X-ray documentation

## 5.D. Complications - Acute

> [!example] Video Breakdown: 6 videos, ~60 minutes

### Video 15: Pneumothorax (10-12 min)
**Incidence**: 1-2% (subclavian approach)
**Symptoms**: Dyspnea, chest pain, hypoxia
**Diagnosis**: CXR (upright), CT if subtle
**Management**:
- Small (<20%) + asymptomatic → observe
- Large or symptomatic → chest tube

### Video 16: Hemothorax & Cardiac Perforation (10-12 min)
**Perforation risk**: 0.5-1%
**Presentation**:
- Acute: Chest pain, hypotension, tamponade
- Delayed: Pericardial effusion 24-72h later
**Echo findings**: Pericardial effusion, lead tip beyond epicardium
**Management**: Pericardiocentesis, lead repositioning vs surgical repair

### Video 17: Lead Dislodgement (8-10 min)
**Incidence**: 2-5% (higher with passive fixation)
**Risk factors**: Atrial leads, patient activity, inadequate fixation
**Presentation**: Loss of capture, sensing failure
**Diagnosis**: X-ray comparison, threshold testing
**Management**: Repositioning (if <48h), replacement (if chronic)

### Video 18: Pneumothorax & Vascular Injury (10-12 min)
**Arterial puncture**: Subclavian artery (1-2%)
- Signs: Pulsatile blood, bright red
- Management: Remove sheath, manual compression, CT angio

**Venous injuries**: SVC tear, brachiocephalic injury
- Rare but life-threatening
- May need sternotomy, vascular repair

### Video 19: Pocket Hematoma (10-12 min)
**Incidence**: 2-5% (higher if anticoagulated)
**Prevention**: Continue warfarin (BRUISE-CONTROL), hold 1 dose DOACs
**Management**:
- Small → observe
- Large/expanding → Consider evacuation (infection risk 5-10×)
- Avoid evacuation unless necessary (rebleeding risk)

### Video 20: Air Embolism (8-10 min)
**Rare but serious**
**Mechanism**: Air enters venous system during lead insertion
**Symptoms**: Sudden hypotension, altered mental status, cardiac arrest
**Prevention**: Keep introducer valve closed, Trendelenburg position
**Treatment**: Hyperbaric oxygen, supportive care

## 5.E. Complications - Chronic

> [!example] Video Breakdown: 5 videos, ~50 minutes

### Video 21: Infection - Pocket vs Lead (10-12 min)
**Incidence**: 1-2% (higher in revisions, diabetes, immunosuppression)
**Types:**
- **Early (<6 months)**: Staph aureus, Staph epidermidis
- **Late (>6 months)**: Often biofilm, indolent

**Management:**
- **ANY device infection** → Complete system extraction + IV antibiotics
- New device: Wait 72h (negative blood cultures), contralateral side

### Video 22: Lead Fracture (10-12 min)
**Timeframe**: Usually >2 years
**Risk factors**: Subclavian crush syndrome, tight ligature, thin patients
**Presentation**: High impedance, oversensing (noise), loss of capture
**Diagnosis**: Impedance trend, fluoroscopy, high-voltage test
**Management**: Lead revision or extraction

### Video 23: Tricuspid Regurgitation (10-12 min)
**Incidence**: 20-30% with transvenous leads (usually mild)
**Mechanism**: Lead through valve, leaflet impingement
**Diagnosis**: Echo (assess severity)
**Management**:
- Mild-moderate: Observe
- Severe: Consider leadless pacemaker, surgical valve repair + epicardial leads

### Video 24: Venous Stenosis/Occlusion (10-12 min)
**Incidence**: 30-50% (often asymptomatic)
**Symptoms**: Arm swelling, collateral veins
**Diagnosis**: Venography, CT
**Management**:
- Symptomatic → Venoplasty, stenting
- Plan ahead for upgrades (use ipsilateral access or contralateral)

### Video 25: Lead Extraction Complications (10-12 min)
**Major complications**: 1-2%
- SVC tear (most common)
- Cardiac avulsion
- Tricuspid valve damage
- Hemothorax

**Requires**:
- High-volume center
- Cardiac surgery backup
- Laser/mechanical sheaths
- Femoral snares for difficult cases

## 5.F. Device Upgrades & Revisions

> [!example] Video Breakdown: 3 videos, ~30 minutes

### Video 26: Upgrade to CRT (10-12 min)
**Indications**: Progressive HF, worsening EF, LBBB development
**Approach**: Add LV lead via existing system
**Challenges**: Venous occlusion (need venoplasty), limited pocket space

### Video 27: Generator Replacement (10-12 min)
**Timing**: ERI reached (elective)
**Procedure**: Open pocket, disconnect old generator, test leads, connect new
**Lead integrity check**: Mandatory (threshold, impedance, sensing)
**Abandoned leads**: Document, monitor

### Video 28: Revision Surgery (8-10 min)
**Indications**: Lead malfunction, upgrade, infection
**Higher risk**: Infection (3× higher than initial), bleeding, venous issues
**Tips**: Minimize re-entry time, pulse lavage, new site if infected
