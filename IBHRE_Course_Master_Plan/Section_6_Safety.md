## 6.A. Electromagnetic Interference (EMI)

> [!example] Video Breakdown: 5 videos, ~50 minutes

### Video 1: EMI Fundamentals (10-12 min)
**Sources of EMI:**
| Source | Frequency | Risk Level | Effect on Device |
|--------|-----------|------------|------------------|
| **60Hz AC power** | 50-60 Hz | Low | Noise reversion mode |
| **Cell phones** | 800-2000 MHz | Very Low | Minimal (keep 6" away) |
| **Anti-theft systems** | Variable | Low | Transient inhibition |
| **MRI** | Strong magnetic + RF | High | See MRI section |
| **Arc welding** | Variable | High | Inhibition, reset |

**Device Responses:**
- **Pacemaker**: Noise reversion (asynchronous pacing VOO/DOO)
- **ICD**: Suspend tachyarrhythmia detection, noise reversion

---

### Video 2: Medical Sources of EMI (10-12 min)
**Electrocautery:**
- Bipolar (safer, localized)
- Monopolar (higher risk, current through body)
- **Management**: Use bipolar when possible, short bursts, monitor ECG

**Radiofrequency Ablation:**
- Very high power (50-100W)
- Keep ablation catheter >2cm from leads
- Monitor for threshold changes post-procedure

**TENS Units & Therapeutic Ultrasound:**
- Generally safe
- Avoid placement directly over device

---

### Video 3: Household & Occupational EMI (8-10 min)
**Generally SAFE:**
- Microwave ovens
- Cell phones (6" away)
- Computers, tablets
- Hair dryers
- Electric razors
- Home appliances

**AVOID or Use with Caution:**
- Arc welding (job restriction)
- Industrial magnets
- Large motors/generators
- Ham radio (high-power transmitters)

---

### Video 4: Security Systems & Screening (10-12 min)
**Airport Security:**
- Metal detectors: Walk through quickly, don't linger
- Full-body scanners: Safe (non-ionizing radiation)
- Hand-held wands: Ask for pat-down instead (strong magnet)

**Anti-Theft Systems (Stores):**
- Walk through quickly
- Don't lean on sensors

**Patient Education:**
- Carry device ID card
- Inform security personnel
- Request manual screening if concerned

---

### Video 5: Magnet Responses & Safety Features (10-12 min)
**Pacemaker Magnet Mode:**
- Asynchronous pacing (VOO/DOO/AOO)
- Rate indicates battery status
- Reversible (remove magnet)

**ICD Magnet Mode:**
- Suspends tachyarrhythmia detection
- Does NOT affect pacing
- Beeping tone confirms detection

**Clinical Uses:**
- Pacemaker: Test capture, determine battery status, EMI environments
- ICD: During surgery, EOL comfort care, inappropriate shocks

## 6.B. MRI Safety

> [!example] Video Breakdown: 4 videos, ~40 minutes

### Video 6: MRI Physics & Risks (10-12 min)
**Three Components:**
1. **Static magnetic field** (1.5T or 3T)
   - Ferromagnetic objects → projectiles
   - Reed switch activation (magnet mode)
2. **Gradient fields** (rapidly changing)
   - Induced currents → heating
3. **Radiofrequency (RF) pulses**
   - Heating at lead tips (most dangerous)

**Historical Concerns:**
- Lead heating (6-25°C rise)
- Induced currents (rapid pacing, VF)
- Reed switch damage
- Torque/dislodgement

---

### Video 7: MRI-Conditional Devices (10-12 min)
**Design Features:**
| Feature | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Non-ferromagnetic materials** | Reduce torque, heating |
| **Modified lead design** | Resistors to limit RF-induced currents |
| **MRI mode programming** | Special settings during scan |
| **Automatic sensing adjustments** | Maintain appropriate sensing during scan |

**MRI-Conditional Labeling:**
- Specific field strength (1.5T or 3T)
- Specific SAR limits (Specific Absorption Rate)
- Body region restrictions (head, chest, extremity)
- Dependent on ENTIRE system (generator + leads)

---

### Video 8: MRI Protocol & Programming (10-12 min)
**Pre-MRI:**
1. Confirm MRI-conditional system (check all components)
2. Interrogate device (baseline parameters)
3. Program to MRI mode:
   - **Pacemaker-dependent**: Asynchronous pacing (DOO, VOO)
   - **Non-dependent**: Inhibited mode (VVI, DDD) or OVO
   - Disable rate response, suspend PMT algorithms
   - **ICD**: Suspend tachyarrhythmia detection

**During MRI:**
- Monitor ECG, pulse oximetry
- Patient communication (call button)

**Post-MRI:**
1. Interrogate device (within 1 month)
2. Resume normal programming
3. Check thresholds (rare changes)

---

### Video 9: Non-Conditional Devices & MRI (10-12 min)
**Off-Label MRI:**
- Not FDA-approved
- Growing evidence of safety (low complication rates)
- Requires experienced team, strict protocol

**Absolute Contraindications:**
- Epicardial/abandoned leads
- Lead integrity issues
- Certain older devices (strong ferromagnetic components)
- Pacemaker-dependent with ventricular arrhythmias

**Risk-Benefit Discussion:**
- MRI necessity (emergency vs elective)
- Alternative imaging (CT, ultrasound, nuclear)
- Extraction + upgrade to MRI-conditional system

## 6.C. Radiation Safety

> [!example] Video Breakdown: 2 videos, ~20 minutes

### Video 10: Radiation Therapy Effects (10-12 min)
**Radiation Dose Effects:**

| Dose (Gy) | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| <2 Gy | Minimal risk |
| 2-10 Gy | Possible device malfunction |
| >10 Gy | High risk of damage (CMOS circuits) |

**Management:**
- Shield device (move if possible)
- Monitor device after each fraction
- Have backup pacing available
- Consider temporary pacing wire if pacemaker-dependent

---

### Video 11: Fluoroscopy & Radiation Exposure (8-10 min)
**Operator Safety:**
- Lead aprons (0.5mm lead equivalent)
- Thyroid shields
- Leaded glasses
- Minimize fluoro time ("pedal off finger off")
- Collimation (reduce field size)

**Patient Exposure:**
- Minimize time, maximize distance
- Use last-frame hold instead of continuous fluoro
- Modern pulsed systems (lower dose)

## 6.D. Electrical Safety

> [!example] Video Breakdown: 2 videos, ~20 minutes

### Video 12: Defibrillation in Device Patients (10-12 min)
**External Cardioversion/Defibrillation:**

> [!warning] Precautions
> - Place pads ≥8cm from device
> - Anteroposterior position preferred
> - Check device after shock (threshold rise risk)

**Backup ICD:**
- If patient has ICD, it should deliver therapy
- External defib if ICD fails
- ICD may interpret external shock as noise → suspend detection temporarily

---

### Video 13: Electrical Hazards & Grounding (8-10 min)
**Microshock vs Macroshock:**
- **Macroshock**: 100mA through body (VF)
- **Microshock**: 100µA directly to heart (via lead) → VF
- Temporary leads = microshock risk

**Operating Room Safety:**
- Grounded equipment
- Isolated power supply
- Avoid multiple ground paths
- Proper cautery pad placement
