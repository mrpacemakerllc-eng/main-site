## 8.A. Chest X-Ray Basics

> [!example] Video Breakdown: 3 videos, ~30 minutes

### Video 1: Standard Views & Anatomy (10-12 min)

**Standard Views:**

| View | Patient Position | What It Shows Best |
|------|------------------|-------------------|
| **PA (Posteroanterior)** | Upright, chest to plate | Overall position, AP relationships |
| **Lateral** | Side view | Anterior/posterior displacement |
| **RAO (Right Anterior Oblique)** | 45° right side forward | RV apex vs septum |
| **LAO (Left Anterior Oblique)** | 45° left side forward | Atrial vs ventricular leads |

**Key Anatomical Landmarks:**

```
PA View:
  - Clavicles (reference for subclavian approach)
  - Ribs (count for lead position)
  - Heart borders (RA, RV, LA, LV outlines)
  - Diaphragm (inferior border)

Lateral View:
  - Sternum (anterior)
  - Spine (posterior)
  - Retrosternal space (RV location)
  - IVC entering RA
```

**Device Identification on X-Ray:**
- Pacemaker: Small, rectangular
- ICD: Larger, visible coils
- CRT: Three leads (RA, RV, CS/LV)
- S-ICD: Subcutaneous electrode along sternum

---

### Video 2: Normal Lead Positions (10-12 min)

**Atrial Lead - RAA Position:**

```
PA View:
  - Points inferiorly and leftward
  - Overlays right heart border
  - Tip at level of diaphragm

Lateral View:
  - Anterior (toward sternum)
  - Curves forward into appendage
```

**RV Apex Lead:**

```
PA View:
  - Points inferiorly and leftward
  - Crosses midline
  - Tip below diaphragm level

Lateral View:
  - Anterior
  - Tip points downward
```

**RV Septal Lead:**

```
PA View:
  - Tip points RIGHTWARD (key difference!)
  - Stays more midline

LAO View:
  - Posterior (toward spine)
  - "Septal bounce" during insertion
```

**LV Lead (via Coronary Sinus):**

```
PA View:
  - Enters CS (medial to RA lead)
  - Courses leftward and inferiorly
  - Tip over left lateral/posterolateral wall

Lateral View:
  - Posterior (toward spine)
  - Distinguishes from RV lead (RV = anterior)
```

---

### Video 3: Lead Slack & Stability Assessment (8-10 min)

**Optimal Lead Slack:**

> [!success] Gentle J-Curve
> - Prevents dislodgement with patient movement
> - Too straight → tension → dislodgement risk
> - Too much slack → loop formation → SVC/RA thrombosis

**Visual Assessment:**

```
Good Slack:
    ╭──────╮
    │      │  Gentle curve
────┘      └───→ Lead tip

Too Straight:
─────────────→ Lead tip (RISK!)

Excessive Slack:
    ╭─────╮
    │  ╭──╯
────┴──╯──→ Lead tip (RISK!)
```

**Active Fixation Confirmation:**
- May see small "pigtail" at tip (helix extended)

## 8.B. Abnormal Lead Positions & Complications

> [!example] Video Breakdown: 4 videos, ~40 minutes

### Video 4: Lead Dislodgement Recognition (10-12 min)

**Atrial Lead Dislodgement:**

```
Normal:
  - RAA: Anterior, inferior, leftward

Dislodged:
  - Retracted into SVC (superior position)
  - Lost anterior curve
  - May be in RA free wall or CS
```

**Ventricular Lead Dislodgement:**

```
Macro-dislodgement:
  - Lead tip moved significantly (cm)
  - May be in RA, RV outflow, or free-floating

Micro-dislodgement:
  - Subtle position change (mm)
  - Compare to baseline X-ray
  - Loss of threshold, sensing suggests dislodgement even if X-ray "looks OK"
```

**Clinical Correlation:**
- Loss of capture (spike without QRS)
- Sensing failure (undersensing)
- Diaphragmatic stimulation (if perforated)

---

### Video 5: Cardiac Perforation (10-12 min)

**X-Ray Signs:**

> [!danger] Lead Perforation
> - Lead tip extends beyond cardiac silhouette
> - Lead "too far" anterior (lateral view)
> - Tip beyond expected location

**Clinical Presentation:**
- Acute: Chest pain, hypotension, tamponade
- Delayed: Pericardial effusion (24-72h later)
- Chronic: Asymptomatic (sealed perforation)

**Echo Confirmation:**
- Pericardial effusion
- Lead tip visualized beyond myocardium

**Management:**
- Small, asymptomatic → Monitor
- Tamponade → Emergent pericardiocentesis
- Pull-back vs surgical removal (depends on timing, stability)

---

### Video 6: Lead Fracture & Hardware Issues (10-12 min)

**X-Ray Signs of Fracture:**

> [!warning] Conductor Fracture
> - May NOT be visible on X-ray (internal break)
> - Impedance trend more sensitive
> - If visible: Discontinuity in conductor coil

**Common Fracture Locations:**
- **Subclavian crush**: Between clavicle and first rib
- **Ligature site**: Where suture tie is too tight
- **Flexion points**: Where lead bends sharply

**Insulation Breach:**
- Usually NOT visible on X-ray
- Diagnosed by impedance (low <200Ω)

**Set Screw Loose:**
- Lead appears disconnected from header
- "Air gap" between pin and port
- Impedance out of range (open circuit)

### Video 7: Pneumothorax & Other Complications (10-12 min)

**Pneumothorax on CXR:**

```
Findings:
  - Loss of lung markings peripherally
  - Visceral pleural line (thin white line)
  - Hyperlucency (darker on affected side)
  - Small: Apical only
  - Large: Entire hemithorax
```

**Size Estimation:**
- Small: <20% (apex only)
- Moderate: 20-50%
- Large: >50% (tension → mediastinal shift)

**Hemothorax:**
- Opacification (white) at lung base
- Blunted costophrenic angle
- Layering fluid on lateral decubitus view

**Lead in Wrong Chamber:**
- LV instead of RV (crosses mitral valve, via VSD or arterial placement)
- CS instead of RV (posterior course)
- Coronary vein perforation (tip too distal)

## 8.C. Advanced Imaging

> [!example] Video Breakdown: 2 videos, ~20 minutes

### Video 8: Fluoroscopy Basics & Lead Manipulation (10-12 min)

**Fluoroscopy Views During Implant:**

| View | Angle | Best For |
|------|-------|----------|
| **AP** | 0° | Overall position, quick checks |
| **RAO** | 30° right | Distinguishing RV apex from septum |
| **LAO** | 40° left | Atrial vs ventricular position |

**Stylet Manipulation:**
- J-curve: Directs lead into RV apex
- Straight: For RAA, septum
- Pre-shaped: For CS cannulation

**CS Cannulation:**
- RAO view: Find CS os (inferior to RA lead)
- Engage with catheter
- Advance LV lead into lateral/posterolateral branch
- Avoid anterior veins (worsen dyssynchrony)

---

### Video 9: CT & MRI Imaging of Devices (10-12 min)

**CT Imaging:**

**Advantages:**
- Excellent spatial resolution
- Multiplanar reconstruction
- Coronary arteries, CS anatomy
- Thrombus, vegetation detection

**Limitations:**
- Artifact from device (metal causes streaks)
- Radiation exposure

**Uses:**
- Pre-CRT planning (CS anatomy)
- Suspected perforation
- Venous obstruction

**MRI Imaging:**

**MRI-Conditional Devices:**
- Safe if entire system is conditional
- Specific field strength (1.5T or 3T)
- Requires MRI mode programming

**What MRI Shows:**
- Scar (late gadolinium enhancement) → VT substrate
- LV function, viability
- Lead position relative to scar

**Artifacts:**
- Leads cause signal void ("black holes")
- Larger artifact from ICDs
